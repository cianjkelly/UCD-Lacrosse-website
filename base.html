WANT TO PLAY LACROSSE?

Looking for a new fun activity to improve your fitness and overall well-being? Or just looking to meet new people in fun and exciting atmosphere? Then UCD lacrosse is the perfect club to join. We are a young, diverse club who accepts all members regardless of skill level or commitment level.

JOINING UCD LACROSSE

Lacrosse has been dubbed "the fastest sport on two feet", and when played at full speed it certainly lives us to its reputation. If you would like to try the sport, then you are more than welcome to attend one of our weekly training sessions - if you e-mail us before hand at lax@ucd.ie we can make sure there will be equipment for you. If you decide that lacrosse is the sport for you, then you can join the club with fees depending on what time of year you decide to join. Fees include training pitch hire, as well as insurance.

ABOUT LACROSSE

Lacrosse is a Native American Iroquois sport, that was used as symbolic warfare to honour the Creator. While the original pitch length could have been anywhere from 500 meters to 3 kilometres long, with teams ranging from 100 to 1,000-a-side, the modern day game boasts a more standard 100m pitch with 11 players on each team for men's lacrosse, and 12 for women's lacrosse.
The game consists of passing and catching the ball in the air with a lacrosse stick (or crosse), to keep possession and ultimately score against the opposition. While the ball is allowed to touch the ground, you are not allowed to handle the ball and it must be picked up using the stick. There are dedicated positions similar to other sports, divided into attack, midfield, defence, and goalkeeper.

Women's Lacrosse

The game is played with 12 players per team on the field (1 goalkeeper). Like other field sports there are different playing positions on the team: attack, midfield, and defence. It is a predominantly non-contact sport.

Men's Lacrosse

It is played with two types of sticks, a field player's stick and a goalkeeper's stick.

The field stick is designed to throw the ball at a fast pace.
The goalkeeper's stick has a wide face to defend the goals.
The "draw" is how play is starts or restarts after a goal.
Checking is when a player uses her own stick to hits an opponent's stick with the aim of turning over possession.
Scoring - goals are scored when the whole ball goes completely over the goal line, within the goal posts (6m x 6m). Play can continue behind the goal line (like ice hockey).
Cradling - as the pocket of the stick is shallow, cradling is a movement that allows the player to move without the ball falling out of the pocket.
